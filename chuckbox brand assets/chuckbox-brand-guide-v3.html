Brand Overview

Chuck Box is the "kitchen drawer" for Scouting units‚Äîeverything you need, organized in one place. Our brand embodies warmth, reliability, and the joy of well-organized adventures. We're friendly, efficient, and built for busy volunteer leaders who need things to just work.

üì¶ Why "Chuck Box"?

A chuck box (or patrol box) is a portable camping kitchen‚Äîa wooden box with compartments for cooking supplies, utensils, spices, and everything a camp cook needs. It's the heart of any campsite kitchen, keeping everything organized so you can focus on the meal, not the mess. That's exactly what we do for Scout unit operations: organize the chaos so leaders can focus on the program.

"Scoutbook is for the Council; Chuck Box is for the Unit."

Usage Guidelines

Minimum Size

Icon alone: 24px minimum. With wordmark: 120px wide minimum to maintain legibility.

Clear Space

Maintain padding equal to the height of the work surface bar around all sides of the logo.

Dark Mode

Swap Pine 800 ‚Üí Pine 600 for box body. Compartments ‚Üí Pine 500. Campfire ‚Üí #F48C06.

Backgrounds

Use light version on white, cream, and light grays. Use dark version on Pine 800 and darker backgrounds.

Logo Do's and Don'ts

Do

Maintain clear space around the logo equal to the height of the work surface bar.

Do

Use the dark background version on forest green or dark backgrounds.

Don't

Stretch, rotate, or distort the logo in any way.

Don't

Change the colors or separate the icon from the wordmark at small sizes.

Chuck Box speaks like your most organized friend‚Äîthe one who always has the right tool and knows where everything is. We're helpful, warm, and occasionally have a bit of fun.

Do say

"All set! 12 requirements synced to Scoutbook. ‚úì"

Don't say

Key Principles

üéØ Be Direct

Volunteer time is precious. Get to the point. Use active voice and simple words.

ü§ó Be Friendly

Use contractions. Add the occasional emoji where appropriate. Sound human, not robotic.

üí° Be Helpful

Anticipate what users need. Offer next steps. Explain errors in plain language.

üîí Be Trustworthy

Never exaggerate. Be transparent about limitations. Protect user data fiercely.
